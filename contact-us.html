Frequently Asked Questions

What does On Edge Safaris do?

We integrate predictive AI, autonomous edge systems, and community-led technical capacity to protect biodiversity hotspots before loss occurs. Our mission is to convert fragmented conservation tools into measurable, climate-grade infrastructure.
What technologies does On Edge Safaris use?

We unify tracking collars, satellite imagery, IoT sensors, patrol systems, radios, and drone networks into one predictive intelligence architecture. Our system combines predictive AI threat modeling, autonomous edge infrastructure, and live biodiversity intelligence.
Where does On Edge Safaris operate?

We are deploying across biodiversity landscapes in Kenya, including areas adjacent to Tsavo National Park and conservancies protecting endangered rhino and elephant populations. Our model is designed for replication across climate-vulnerable biodiversity corridors globally.
What is the Eco-Tech Guardians program?

We train women as drone operators, sensor technicians, and data analysts, embedding local technical capacity into conservation infrastructure. This builds trusted community relationships that underpin landscape-scale deployment.
How does On Edge Safaris support climate finance?

By reducing degradation and stabilizing ecosystems, we safeguard natural carbon sinks, water systems, and biodiversity assets. Our work strengthens carbon permanence, enhances ecosystem resilience, and supports climate-finance transparency.
How do I get in touch with On Edge Safaris?

Reach out through our contact form. We’ll welcome partnerships with conservancies, climate investors, and organizations committed to protecting biodiversity.
